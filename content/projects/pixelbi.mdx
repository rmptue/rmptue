## what it is

Roughly 22 pixel-art works and animated shorts produced from 2019 to 2020 during my undergrad at UPLB. No AI involved — that&apos;s the point.

## why it&apos;s here

Every other project on this site involves a model doing some of the work. Freedom Park&apos;s asset pipeline reads as &quot;AI-collaborative&quot; only if there&apos;s evidence that the human can do the work without the AI. Pixelbi is that evidence.

It&apos;s the credibility anchor. Before AI-assisted pipelines, this is what manual pixel art looked like in my hands. The Freedom Park pipeline is enhancement, not crutch.

## the work

Hand-pixeled in Aseprite and Photoshop. UPLB campus scenes, character studies, short looping animations. The animations are the part I&apos;m proudest of — frame-by-frame work on idle cycles and ambient motion.

## what i learned

Doing the manual version first matters. A lot of AI-assisted artists I&apos;ve seen never did the manual version, and it shows in their judgment about what the model gets wrong. They can&apos;t tell when the proportions are off or when the lighting language breaks, because they don&apos;t have the muscle memory.

The credibility argument runs both ways. The manual work proves I can judge model output. The AI-assisted work proves I can scale beyond what the manual version&apos;s asset budget would allow. Either alone is weaker.
