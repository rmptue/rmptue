## what it is

Solo environmental analytics consultancy. Public-facing site at [altographanalytics.com](https://altographanalytics.com) and a flagship credibility artifact — the CCET (Climate Change Expenditure Tagging) Explorer, a public visualization of the Philippine national climate budget.

This one earns its slot on the AI portfolio through the public artifact and the build process behind it, not by being the most AI-heavy project on the page.

## the AI angle

Claude Code does most of the development assistance across the consultancy stack:

- data-pipeline scaffolding for new client engagements
- MRV (monitoring, reporting, verification) analysis drafting
- SEO content generation for the consultancy site
- public-explorer rebuilds (CCET v0.2 four-view redesign currently in flight)

The pattern is &quot;Claude Code as a senior contractor I direct.&quot; I scope the work, write the brief, review the output, and ship. The model handles the tedium; I handle the judgment about what we&apos;re actually building.

## CCET Explorer

V0.1 is live. V0.2 is in flight — a four-view redesign that lets users slice the budget by agency, region, climate strategy, and program. The interesting work isn&apos;t the data (the dataset is small); it&apos;s the question of what views actually surface useful patterns to a non-technical reader. V0.2 is the version where I take that seriously.

## why this is on the page

Two reasons.

One: it&apos;s public. A portfolio with no public artifact reads weaker than one with public artifacts, regardless of how impressive the private work is. The CCET Explorer is something a reader can click and judge.

Two: it&apos;s evidence of the AI-assisted pipeline working at consultancy scale, not just personal-build scale. Different audience, different deliverable, same pattern of human-direction-plus-model-execution.

## what i learned

The right unit for AI-assisted development at consultancy scale is the brief, not the prompt. A good brief turns into many good prompts. A good prompt without a good brief turns into a polished thing nobody asked for.

Public artifacts compound. The CCET Explorer was built once and now it&apos;s a permanent piece of context for every conversation I have about climate analytics. That&apos;s a much better return than the time it took to ship.
