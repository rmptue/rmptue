## chief of staff assessment — venture studio

Took an applied-AI Chief of Staff assessment for a venture studio. Three deliverables in 48 hours, with Claude as my analyst across all three.

The framing question for this case study is **what Claude did vs. what I did**. Most CoS work is judgment-heavy; the interesting thing is which sub-tasks AI handles cleanly and which need human editorial.

## deliverable 1 — growth funnel rebuild

A landing-page variant plus three ad-creative angles plus a testing plan for a portfolio company in a specific vertical (anonymized). The brief was &quot;double their qualified-lead rate within current ad spend.&quot;

**Claude did:** angle generation (the model is fast at producing seven plausible angles for a given audience-and-offer combination), copy ideation for the landing-page variant, draft testing-plan structure with sample sizes and success criteria.

**I did:** selection — which of seven angles actually fits the studio&apos;s thesis and the portco&apos;s positioning, and the judgment that the landing-page friction was a higher-leverage fix than the ad creative. Also the testing plan&apos;s sequencing, because the model defaulted to &quot;test everything in parallel&quot; which would have starved each variant of traffic.

## deliverable 2 — LinkedIn talent sourcing methodology

A search-query design plus three candidate profiles plus outreach scripts for a Head of Sales role at another portco.

**Claude did:** outreach drafts in three voice registers (warm, direct, peer-to-peer), candidate-profile synthesis from public LinkedIn data, search-query variants for the source layer.

**I did:** voice tuning — the warm register the model produced read as performatively warm, which is a tell for sales ops people, so I rewrote it dryer. Also the methodology framing — the model wanted to lead with &quot;here&apos;s how to find good candidates,&quot; which is the wrong framing for an audience that already knows how to find candidates. The methodology had to lead with what was *different* about the search criteria for this role.

## deliverable 3 — five-minute strategic walkthrough

A recorded Loom synthesizing the above two deliverables with a strategic framing.

**Claude did:** outline of the walkthrough with timing per section, draft narration for the funnel deliverable (which I rewrote heavily for voice), checklist of what to point at on screen.

**I did:** all the strategic framing, all the spoken delivery, the decision to compress the talent deliverable into 90 seconds and spend the saved budget on the funnel walkthrough, which was the more thesis-relevant deliverable for this studio.

## what the breakdown shows

Claude is good at: angle generation, draft prose, structured outputs against a clear spec, candidate synthesis from public data, plausible alternatives.

Claude is not as good at: positioning judgment, audience-fit calibration, &quot;what should we even be testing&quot; calls, voice tuning past surface register, sequencing with a real budget constraint.

The AI-assisted CoS isn&apos;t doing 100% of the work. It&apos;s doing 60–70% of the work and freeing the human to spend their time on the 30–40% that the model can&apos;t do credibly. That&apos;s the productivity argument, and it&apos;s a more honest one than the &quot;Claude is your chief of staff&quot; framing.

## what i learned

The CoS role is judgment-dense. The model accelerates the parts adjacent to judgment — research, drafting, alternative generation — but the judgment itself doesn&apos;t delegate well. That&apos;s a useful boundary to know going in, and it&apos;s the boundary that decides whether you&apos;re using the model well or theatrically.
